Lessons

There are a large, finite, number of lessons that could be learned and executed within the game of Chess. These are just 6 lessons that will teach you some of these lessons and hopefully you'll go out and explore the enormous world of chess.

Piece Value

In Chess every piece has a "value" assigned to it in the form of a points system. This point system allows players to evaluate a situation and see if they're losing or winning (in terms of points).

Castling

You can achieve both the activation of a Rook and aquire King safety in one move. This is a very important action if you want to remain relevant with your opponent.

Fundamentals of Openings

There are three fundamentals of the opening phase. These define weither a opening is good or bad for the side. This can also help you decide on the moves that you would want to do in the opening if you don't know too much about standard openings.

Exchanging

Every scenario has a potenital to trade pieces because of positional motive, sacrifice points for position, etc. A good player will see when to trade, and what to trade in these positions.

Good and Bad Pieces

Even though a black bishop is worth the same amount of points as a white bishop, their positions can change how much they're worth in the eyes of players. Sometimes moving the rook one tile is worth more than a entire queen. See which pieces are worth more than others, and capitalize on these opportunities to become a great chess player.

King and Pawn Endgames

Endgames are very interesting becuase of their mechanical nature, one wrong king move can lose you the game. Learn how to play certain endgames where you're up one pawn to the opponent.
